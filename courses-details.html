Course Structure

The course covers foundational and advanced topics necessary for passing the CPC exam and working as a medical coder.

  • Introduction to Medical Coding
    • Overview of healthcare systems, terminology, and coding fundamentals
    • Basics of medical billing and coding
  • ICD-10-CM (International Classification of Diseases, 10th Edition, Clinical Modification):
    • Understanding diagnosis coding systems
    • Learning how to use the ICD-10-CM codes for medical diagnoses
  • CPT (Current Procedural Terminology) Coding:
    • Introduction to procedural coding using CPT codes
    • Understanding the different sections of CPT (Evaluation and Management, Anesthesia, Surgery, Radiology, Pathology, and Medicine)
  • HCPCS Level II (Healthcare Common Procedure Coding System):
    • Coverage of supplies, drugs, and durable medical equipment codes
  • Modifiers:
    • Explanation of modifier codes and their application to medical billing and coding
  • Compliance and Legal Aspects:
    • Understanding of HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) regulations
    • Medical ethics and compliance issues in coding
  • Reimbursement and Billing Processes:
    • Overview of insurance claims, reimbursement systems, and payment structures
    • Medicare and Medicaid billing specifics

Examination Preparation

  • CPC Exam Preparation:
    • Practice exams and mock tests to simulate real exam conditions
    • Review of test-taking strategies and tips for managing time
    • Study materials like CPC Exam study guides, practice questions, and coding books
  • Sample Case Studies and Real-World Scenarios:
    • Hands-on coding exercises using actual medical records
    • Case studies to help students understand the application of codes in real-world situations
